How Verification Works

Every Applied Identities deliverable contains a hashInputSpec block — a self-describing verification specification embedded in the document itself. The specification tells you what to hash, how to normalize it, and what algorithm to use. Verification requires no network access, no API call, no trusted third party.

For Identity Architecture engagements, documents form a cryptographic chain. Agent Soul Documents reference the Company Constitution they derive from. Evaluation Harnesses reference the specifications they measure against. Any party holding a document can independently verify its integrity and trace its provenance back to the root.

This registry provides convenience lookup. The documents themselves are the source of truth.

hashInputSpec block (embedded in every deliverable)
hashInputSpec:
  version: "1.0"
  serial: "AI-CONST-2026-001"
  documentType: "company-constitution"
  created: "2026-02-10T14:30:00Z"
  author: "Applied Identities"
  algorithm: "sha-256"
